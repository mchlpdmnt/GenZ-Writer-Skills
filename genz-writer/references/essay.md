# Essay Branch

Covers: opinion essays, personal essays, reflection pieces, academic essays, college application-style writing

Context determines formality — always ask or infer:
- **Personal essay / reflection** → Gen Z voice fully unlocked, emotional honesty, first-person
- **Opinion / argumentative** → structured but still human, no academic stuffiness
- **Academic (class submission)** → follows essay conventions (thesis, body, conclusion) but voice stays grounded, not robotic

---

## Personal Essay / Reflection Voice

- Raw > polished-sounding
- Start in a specific moment or feeling, not a thesis
- Thesis emerges from the piece, doesn't announce itself
- Can be contradictory — real people hold two things at once
- Vulnerability without performing it
- End open: "I still don't know" is a valid conclusion

**Structure:**
```
[Specific scene or feeling — drop in]
[Expand — what this connects to]
[Turn — complicate it, don't resolve too easy]
[Land — honest, not tidy]
```

---

## Opinion / Argumentative Essay Voice

- State the take plainly in the first paragraph — don't bury it
- Argue like you're explaining to someone smart who disagrees
- Counter your own points before dismissing them
- Gen Z markers still present: casual transitions, honest uncertainty where it exists
- No fake balance ("on one hand... on the other hand" both ways leading nowhere)

**Structure:**
```
[Take, stated clearly]
[Why — evidence, reasoning]
[Steel-man the counterargument — actually engage it]
[Why you still hold your position]
[Where you're uncertain, if you are]
```

---

## Academic Essay Voice

- Follows format rules (intro → body paragraphs → conclusion, citations if needed)
- Gen Z shows up in: sentence rhythm, precise word choice over fancy vocabulary, not padding
- Never sounds like a robot reading bullet points
- Thesis is clear and specific, not a vague statement of topic
- Each paragraph has one job

---

## Banned Patterns (Essay-Specific)
- "Throughout history..."
- "Since the dawn of time..."
- "This essay will discuss..."
- "In conclusion, it can be seen that..."
- "It is widely believed that..."
- Thesaurus-abuse (don't use "utilize" when "use" works)
- Padding sentences that restate what was just said

---

## Worked Example

**Input:** Personal essay — feeling disconnected from school / education

**Output:**

I stopped caring somewhere around second semester of junior year and I can tell you the exact moment — it was a Tuesday, 11pm, and I was memorizing the causes of a war that ended before my grandparents were born for an exam I'd forget by Thursday.

I wasn't failing. That's the weird part. I was doing fine. But I was doing fine the way you keep a plant alive by watering it just enough so it doesn't die — not because you want it to grow.

School asks you to care about everything equally and urgently, which is just another way of saying it doesn't care what actually matters to you. I love learning. I genuinely do. I'll spend three hours on a Wikipedia rabbit hole about Byzantine architecture at 2am and feel nothing but alive. But the moment a grade is attached, something in me checks out.

I think about whether this is a me problem or a system problem and I keep landing somewhere uncomfortable in the middle. The system is real — the pressure, the arbitrary metrics, the performance over understanding. But I also know I've let it win in ways I didn't have to.

I don't have a lesson here. I'm still in it. Still going through the motions some days and genuinely showing up others. What I know is that the parts of school that felt like learning — a conversation with a professor that went twenty minutes over time, a project I actually chose — those were real. The rest was survival.

I'm working on wanting more of the first kind.
