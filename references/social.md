# Social Branch

Covers: Twitter/X posts & threads, Instagram captions, TikTok captions

---

## Voice Rules

- Lowercase by default unless emphasis demands caps (e.g. "I AM NORMAL")
- Emojis: 0–3 max. placed naturally, not as decoration. 😭 and 💀 are valid punctuation
- No hashtags unless user specifically asks
- Contractions always. "I am" → "I'm", "it is" → "it's"
- Write like you're texting someone who gets it

---

## Structure by Platform

### Twitter/X — Single Post
- 1–3 sentences max
- Hook is the whole thing — no setup, no landing
- Can end mid-thought for effect

### Twitter/X — Thread
- First tweet: the take, no context. Make them want to keep reading.
- Middle tweets: each one a standalone thought, builds on the last
- Last tweet: landing or open question — NOT a summary, NOT "that's all folks"
- Format: number each tweet `1/`, `2/`, etc.

### Instagram Caption
- First line does the heavy lifting (it's what shows before "more")
- Can be longer than Twitter but still tight
- Personal > universal
- No "link in bio" or any CTA unless asked

### TikTok Caption
- Ultra short. 1 sentence or a punchy fragment.
- Can be chaotic on purpose

---

## Banned Patterns (Social-Specific)
- "This is your sign to..."
- "Not me [verb]-ing..."  ← overused, ban unless it's genuinely funny
- "POV: you..."
- "I don't know who needs to hear this but..."
- Fake-humble flex openers
- Ending with a question just to farm engagement ("What do you think? Drop it below!")

## Worked Example

**Input:** Topic — school feeling pointless, raw topic

**Output (Twitter single post):**
> school really feels like a side quest that somehow became the main storyline. the rewards are just stress and deadlines and at this point I'm not even studying to learn anymore, I'm studying to survive 😭

**Output (Thread):**
> 1/ school really feels like a side quest that somehow became the main storyline
>
> 2/ like why am I memorizing things I'll Google in 2 seconds anyway. what is this actually preparing me for
>
> 3/ the worst part is I used to genuinely like learning. somewhere between the deadlines and the grading I just. stopped caring
>
> 4/ I'm not failing. I'm doing fine technically. but I'm not there anymore and that's a different kind of exhausting
