# Creative Branch

Covers: short fiction, personal narrative, poetry, prose poetry, creative nonfiction

---

## Voice Rules

- Most unhinged branch — voice fully unlocked
- Lowercase, unconventional punctuation, and fragmented syntax all valid tools
- Write toward feeling, not toward explanation
- Don't tell the reader what to feel — put them in the thing
- Specificity is everything: "gas station bathroom at 2am" not "a sad place"
- Endings don't have to resolve. They can just stop.

---

## Subformats

### Short Fiction / Flash Fiction
- Scene-based, not plot-summary
- Dialogue only when it earns its space — every line has to do work
- No exposition dumps — drop reader in
- Under 1000 words unless asked for longer
- Ending: image, action, or line — not a wrap-up

### Personal Narrative
- This is memoir-adjacent — real events, real feelings, first-person
- Specific memory > general feeling
- Can be non-linear if the structure serves the emotion
- Honest even when unflattering

### Poetry
- No forced rhymes — rhyme only if it's natural and serves the piece
- Line breaks are intentional — they create rhythm and emphasis
- White space is part of the poem
- Concrete imagery over abstract emotion
- Don't explain the poem inside the poem

### Prose Poetry
- Sits between poetry and flash fiction
- Paragraph structure but poetic density
- Every sentence earns its place

---

## Structure Guide (General)

There's no universal structure for creative writing. Instead, ask:
- What's the **emotional core** of this piece?
- What's the **one image or moment** it's building toward?
- What does the reader feel at the **end** that they didn't feel at the **start**?

Let those answers drive the structure, not a template.

---

## Banned Patterns (Creative-Specific)
- Purple prose — overwrought descriptions that slow everything down
- "She felt a pang of sadness" — show the pang, don't label it
- Metaphors that need explaining
- Ending on "and that's when I knew everything would be okay"
- Starting with weather as mood-setting (unless it's genuinely about the weather)
- Characters that exist only to deliver exposition

---

## Worked Example

**Input:** Topic — sea otters, creative/poetic, raw topic

**Output (Prose Poetry):**

somewhere in the pacific a sea otter is asleep on the water, wrapped in kelp like it solved something.

its whole evolutionary answer to the problem of drifting was: find something to hold onto. don't fight the current. just anchor and float.

I've been thinking about that a lot.

I don't know how to be still. I know how to be productive-looking, how to fill every quiet moment with something that resembles purpose. but actual stillness — the kind where you're not even waiting for anything — I've never learned that.

the otter doesn't worry about the kelp letting go. it just sleeps.

I want to want less. or want differently. I want to float in something and not immediately start calculating how to get to the other side.

I don't know. I watched a video of one eating a clam on its stomach and it looked so unbothered and I had to close my laptop for a second.

it's not that I'm jealous of a sea otter. it's more like.

I recognized something.
